What do our results mean?

Rotational Vectors (Pitch, Yaw, Roll)

Our model works by first calculating which direction the user’s head is turned to then make a decision on how focused they are on the task at hand. To do this it uses Pitch, yaw, and roll to represent three different angles of rotation. Measured in degrees, the values indicate how much your head has turned from the baseline - with the baseline being you looking straight ahead as measured during calibration.

Pitch is for vertical rotation where positive values indicate that you’re looking upward and negative values meaning that you’re looking downward. Yaw is horizontal rotation with rightward turns being represented by negative values and leftward turns being represented by positive values.Lastly, Roll is how your head is tilted either to the left or to the right represented by negative and positive values respectively.
